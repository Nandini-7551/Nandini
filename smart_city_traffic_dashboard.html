// 01 — INTRODUCTION
About This Dashboard
India records over 1.5 lakh road deaths annually, making it one of the most road-accident-prone nations globally. The UrbanPulse Smart City Traffic Analytics Dashboard addresses this crisis by providing data-driven visibility into accident patterns, causes, temporal peaks, and risk scores across Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Pune, and Chandigarh.
DATASET OVERVIEW
CSV · 20K ROWS

The dataset (indian_roads_dataset.csv) contains 20,000 road accident records with 24 features per incident, including geolocation coordinates, accident severity, weather conditions, time of occurrence, cause classification, risk scores, and city metadata.

// 02 — HOW TO USE
Dashboard Navigation Guide
This dashboard is designed for traffic analysts, urban planners, law enforcement, and smart-city administrators. Each section serves a distinct purpose in the traffic intelligence workflow.
01

KPI COMMAND STRIP

The five glowing metric cards at the top give you an instant city-wide health snapshot — total incidents, fatalities, average AI risk score, peak danger hour, and severity split. Start every session here to understand the macro state.

The animated road network visualises live traffic congestion levels. Cyan roads are smooth, yellow is moderate, red is heavy. Watch vehicle dots and pulsing hotspots to identify critical corridors that need rerouting or signal intervention.

// 04 — KEY HIGHLIGHTS
Critical Findings at a Glance
The most significant patterns, anomalies, and actionable insights extracted from the 20,000-record analysis.
🕑

THE 2 AM ANOMALY

888

The single deadliest hour in the dataset is 2:00 AM with 888 incidents — more than any morning or evening rush window. Night-time fatigue, drunk driving, and reduced law enforcement create a perfect storm after midnight.

⚖️

5 CAUSES — EQUAL WEIGHT

~20%

All five accident causes — distraction, overspeeding, weather, drunk driving, and poor roads — each account for exactly ~20% of incidents. This statistical equality proves road safety is a systemic, multi-factor crisis requiring simultaneous interventions.

🏙️

URBAN ROADS MOST LETHAL

1,039

Urban roads generate more fatal accidents (1,039) than highways (983) or rural roads (965). Dense intersections, mixed traffic, pedestrian exposure, and inadequate signals make city roads the most dangerous per incident category.

🌧️

RAIN KILLS MORE THAN FOG

1,000

Rain conditions produce the highest fatality count (1,000) followed by fog (995) — yet total incident counts across all three weather types are nearly identical. Rain increases lethal outcomes even when collision frequency stays constant.

📅

MONDAYS MOST DANGEROUS

2,966

Monday leads all days with 2,966 incidents — disproving the common assumption that weekends are most dangerous. Start-of-week fatigue, heavy commercial vehicle movement, and post-holiday reentry driving contribute to Monday's elevated risk.

🤖

NO SAFE CITY EXISTS

0.008

The AI risk score spread between the safest city (Mumbai, 0.434) and riskiest (Chandigarh, 0.442) is just 0.008 — a negligible margin. All 8 Indian metros carry elevated risk, meaning nationwide infrastructure and policy reform is needed rather than city-specific fixes.

// 05 — CONCLUSION
Summary & Recommendations
What the data tells us — and what India's smart cities must do next.
Final Analysis — UrbanPulse Traffic Intelligence Report

What the Data Tells Us

The 20,000-incident Indian roads dataset paints a sobering picture of road safety across eight of India's largest cities. There is no single villain — distraction, speed, weather, alcohol, and infrastructure failure each contribute equally at approximately 20%, making this a deeply systemic problem.


The temporal analysis challenges conventional wisdom: accidents peak at 2 AM, not during rush hour, and Mondays are more dangerous than weekends. Urban roads are deadlier per incident than highways, and no city achieves a "safe" risk score — all eight metros cluster between 0.434 and 0.442 on the AI risk index.

Key Recommendations

  • Deploy night-time enforcement units between midnight and 4 AM when incidents peak, particularly on highways into Mumbai and Kolkata.
  • Implement variable speed limits triggered by real-time rain and fog detection to reduce the 1,000 rain-related fatalities.
  • Launch Monday morning campaigns targeting commercial vehicle operators and commuters who face the highest start-of-week risk.
  • Prioritise urban road infrastructure repair as a fatality reduction strategy — poor roads account for 20% of all incidents.
  • Build AI-driven signal optimisation at high-density urban intersections to reduce the 1,039 urban fatal accidents annually.

Dashboard Impact

UrbanPulse demonstrates how data visualisation can transform raw accident records into operational intelligence. Traffic control centres using this dashboard can monitor congestion in real time, predict high-risk windows 6 hours ahead, and dispatch emergency resources proactively rather than reactively.


The AI risk gauge and forecast module provide the decision-making layer that bridges analytics and action — turning 20,000 historical data points into a living safety management system.

Future Scope

  • Integrate live GPS feeds from vehicles and traffic cameras for true real-time incident detection.
  • Expand to 50+ tier-2 cities including Nagpur, Jaipur, Lucknow, and Ahmedabad for national coverage.
  • Add machine learning prediction models trained on historical patterns to improve the congestion forecast accuracy beyond 6 hours.
  • Implement emergency vehicle routing integration to automatically clear corridors when incident alerts fire.
  • Develop a citizen-facing mobile app that delivers personalised route risk scores before a journey begins.
